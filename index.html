Fictional Historical Profile

Stanley Rawlinson (1789–1854)

Surveyor, cartographer, and amateur astronomer of the Surrey Hills. Remembered for meticulous parish maps, measured mile stones, and star‑charts drawn by lantern‑light.

Born

Michaelmas, 1789 — Dorking parish, Surrey

Apprenticed to a local surveyor at thirteen; learned trigonometry from dog‑eared notebooks and the night sky.

Notable Work

1814–1837

Chart of the Mole Valley; a set of parish boundary maps; a proposed standard for mile‑stone spacing along coaching roads.

Interests

Astronomy · Geodesy · Local lore

Operated a backyard observatory with a handmade 3‑inch refractor; kept almanacs noting comets, frosts, and floods.

About Stanley

Stanley Rawlinson is a fictional figure created for creative and educational projects. Set against the early 19th‑century English countryside, his story blends practical surveying with quiet stargazing. He represents the many anonymous craftspeople whose careful measurements underpinned railways, canals, and parish life.

“A yard unmeasured is a tale untold; the land keeps truth as the heavens keep time.” — attributed to Rawlinson, notebook fragment (fictional)

Timeline (Fictional)

  1. 1789 · Born near Dorking; mother a lace‑maker, father a wheelwright.
  2. 1802 · Apprenticed to Mrs. Harbury, surveyor; first triangulation from Box Hill to Ranmore.
  3. 1814 · Completes Chart of the Mole Valley; praised for its legibility and consistent mile marks.
  4. 1821 · Builds a modest observatory; begins nightly star logs of Orion and the Pleiades.
  5. 1830 · Publishes pamphlet on boundary stones; advocates public posting of parish surveys.
  6. 1837 · Commissioned to advise on a proposed coaching road realignment; retires soon after due to ill‑health.
  7. 1854 · Passes away at home; notebooks bequeathed to a village school (all fictional).

Chart of the Mole Valley

Large‑format manuscript map with neat hachures and hand‑tinted waterways. Notes on flood‑meadows and toll‑gates in the margin.

Lantern Almanac

Pocket notebook logging frost depths, planting days, and comet sightings; includes a simple equation of time table.

Boundary Stones Pamphlet

Argues for durable marks at parish edges and standardized inscriptions to reduce disputes.
