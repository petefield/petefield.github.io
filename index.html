Stanley Rawlinson (1789–1854)

Adventurer, Diplomat, Politician

About Stanley

Sir Stanley Rawlinson, KCB, OM, FRS (1814-1914) is one of British history’s most quietly influential, yet unrecognised figures. Serving his country in fields military, diplomatic and political, Sir Stanley frequently found himself playing a minor role in events that would shape the world around him.

But in a time when the heroism (and indeed villainy) of British adventurers was commonplace, and reported widely in the pages of dailies and periodicals, it is perhaps inevitable that some remain household names, whilst others fade into the background.

Sir Stanley is one of the latter. With no direct descendants to ensure his name is stamped upon his achievements, his life has risked becoming nothing more than a footnote in history.

The Stanley Rawlinson Society was established in 2016 by an enthusiastic group of amateur historians with a mission to give Sir Stanley the recognition that he deserves. The SRS was given a boost in 2023 with the death of Sir Stanley’s last known descendents Charles Rutherford-Rawlinson. Learning only of the existence of the SRS shortly before his passing, Charles bequeathed the society a small sum to cover administrative costs, but more importantly, a number of documents, letters and photographs related to Sir Stanley and his family.

Society enthusiasts are now diligently working through the SRS archive, cross-referencing with official records, and hope to update this website soon. In time, we hope that Sir Stanley will take his rightful place in the pantheon of Victorian statesmen, adventurers and explorers.

Timeline (Fictional)

  1. 1789 · Born near Dorking; mother a lace‑maker, father a wheelwright.
  2. 1802 · Apprenticed to Mrs. Harbury, surveyor; first triangulation from Box Hill to Ranmore.
  3. 1814 · Completes Chart of the Mole Valley.
  4. 1821 · Builds a modest observatory; begins nightly star logs.
  5. 1830 · Publishes pamphlet on boundary stones.
  6. 1854 · Passes away at home.
